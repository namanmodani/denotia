Frequently Asked Questions

> What is Denotia?

Denotia is a web-based application that uses an image-classifying machine learning model to predict rare neurological diseases from brain scans, such as FTLD.

> How common is FTLD?

Despite being a rare disease (NORD link), it is the second most common form of dementia for people under 65. It usually occurs between 40 to 75 and often goes misdiagnosed/undiagnosed. As it is irreversible, it is important to classify it at an early stage to ensure specific interventions, timely management with symptomatic treatment, and caregiver sensitization.

> How do I use Denotia?

Simple! Just go to our homepage, upload your scan and click upload. Once processed, you'll be able to view the algorithm's prediction.

> How does Denotia work?

Our machine-learning model was built on Python and trained using scans from FTLDNI, the largest database for FTD scans. It has learnt to recognize surrogate markers of FTLD and continuously improves, thereby aiding in diagnosis of the disorder.
